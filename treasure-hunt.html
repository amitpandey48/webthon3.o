About Tech Treasure Hunt

Get ready for the most exciting and challenging treasure hunt that combines technology, problem-solving, and adventure! The Tech Treasure Hunt at Nirvana 2.0 is an innovative outdoor activity where teams navigate through campus locations, solve technology-related puzzles, complete coding challenges, and decode digital clues to find hidden treasures.

This unique event tests not only your technical knowledge but also teamwork, communication skills, and creative thinking. Teams will use smartphones, QR codes, GPS navigation, and various tech tools to progress through multiple checkpoints and ultimately discover the grand treasure.

Hunt Format & Structure

Team Formation

Teams of 3-5 members work together to solve challenges and navigate through checkpoints

  • Mixed skill teams encouraged
  • Each team gets unique starting clues
  • Team collaboration is essential

Multiple Checkpoints

10 strategically placed checkpoints across campus with different tech challenges

  • QR code scanning challenges
  • Programming puzzles
  • Hardware identification tasks
  • Tech trivia questions

Progressive Difficulty

Challenges increase in complexity as teams advance through the hunt

  • Beginner-friendly starting challenges
  • Advanced coding problems later
  • Bonus challenges for extra points

Final Challenge

Ultimate treasure location revealed through final mega-puzzle

  • Combine clues from all checkpoints
  • Solve master encryption puzzle
  • Race to the treasure location

Challenge Categories

QR Code Puzzles

Scan QR codes hidden around campus to unlock digital clues and coded messages

Location-based QR hunts Encrypted message decoding Sequential QR challenges

Programming Challenges

Solve coding problems on-the-go using mobile coding apps or laptops

Algorithm puzzles Debug broken code Complete function implementations

Tech Trivia

Answer technology-related questions covering hardware, software, and industry knowledge

Hardware identification Programming language facts Tech company history

GPS Navigation

Use GPS coordinates and mapping tools to find specific campus locations

Coordinate-based navigation Landmark identification Distance calculations

Cryptography

Decode encrypted messages using various cipher techniques and digital tools

Caesar cipher decoding Binary message conversion Hash function challenges

Network Puzzles

Solve network-related challenges involving IP addresses, protocols, and connections

IP address calculations Port scanning simulation Protocol identification

Rules & Guidelines

  • Teams must consist of 3-5 members
  • All team members must stay together during the hunt
  • Smartphones or tablets required for each team
  • No internet searches allowed for specific challenge answers
  • Teams must follow designated campus paths and routes
  • Time penalties apply for rule violations
  • Help from non-team members is not permitted
  • All checkpoints must be completed in any order
  • Final submission deadline is strictly enforced
  • Judges' decisions are final

Eligibility & Requirements

  • Open to all students and professionals
  • Mixed age groups welcome in teams
  • Basic programming knowledge recommended but not required
  • Teams must have at least one smartphone with GPS
  • Comfortable walking shoes and weather-appropriate clothing
  • Registration fee: $50 per team
  • Maximum 50 teams can participate
  • Registration closes 24 hours before event
